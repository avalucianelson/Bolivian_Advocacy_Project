Behind Every Brazil Nut: A Journey of Hands and Hearts

An inside look at the dedication, effort, and generations of tradition behind the families who harvest Brazil nuts in the Amazon Rainforest, made possible by the photo journalism of Bolivian photographer Tyfano Peter.

What's so Special About Brazil Nuts?

What seems like an ordinary snack hides a remarkable story of resilience and tradition. Recognizing the labor and tradition behind each Brazil nut invites a deeper appreciation for this exceptional resource and encourages mindful consumption.

Quick Facts

  • The name "Brazil Nut" is a bit of a misnomer ‚Äî Brazil nuts are actually a seed, and most Brazil nuts are harvested in Bolivia! (Source)
  • Brazil nuts are the most economically impactful non-timber forest product in the Amazon Basin. (Source)
  • The Brazil nut industry supports more than 60,000 families in Brazil, Peru, and Bolivia. (Source)
  • Some Brazil nut trees have been radiocarbon dated as 1000 years old! (Source)
  • As of 2022, the top importer of Brazil nuts was the USA, importing a whopping $69.8 million worth. Bolivia was the world's top exporter, exporting $144 million worth of Brazil nuts! (Source)
